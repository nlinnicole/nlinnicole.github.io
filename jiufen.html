Jiufen Street



Introduction

This scene was inspired by a photo I took while traveling in Jiufen, Taiwan. I loved how lush and green the scenery was! While creating the concept for this scene and sketching out a plan, I actually ended up finding the location on google maps to gather more reference.

My goal for this project was to create a stylized watercolor-inspired scene. I wanted to create a shader for watercolor-style shadows, get more comfortable using Substance Designer and creating smart materials for Substance Painter, and to practice creating 3D foliage.


Watercolor Texture

The first material I made in Substance Designer was a watercolor texture I could turn into a smart material to use for texturing in Substance Painter. First, I created 3 different brushstrokes using Photoshop, then they were layered together with the ability to independently adjust the color, scale, and opacity. I also recreated a version of this as a shader to use in-engine. The shader version was used for larger areas such as the ground and walls.


Brick Texture

I used the watercolor material again in the brick texture, which gave a lot of nice subtle color variation. I wanted to make sure that the grout of the brick had variation in the lines, like it was imperfectly painted with a paintbrush.


Unique Textures

I textured the unique meshes such as the moped, plant pots, and gardening supplies using Substance Painter. I used the watercolor smart material again then added some AO, curvature, and sometimes a positional gradient. I used more noise and brushstroke-like textures for these masks to break them up and give them a more painterly, organic feel.
MOPED, PLANT POTS, GARDENING SUPPLIES

Trim Sheets

I made a couple other trim sheets using a combination of Substance Designer and baking them from Blender and texturing in Substance Painter.
TRIM TEXTURED MESHES (TABLE, CRATE, ELECTRIC POLE)

Foliage

I painted the plant textures in Photoshop then arranged the foliage planes in Blender. To get more color variation, I duplicated the material in Unity with a subtle tint so there is the original version and a darker version of the foliage.
